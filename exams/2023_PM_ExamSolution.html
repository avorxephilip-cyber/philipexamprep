2023 Project Management Exam Solutions

2023 EXAMINATION — SECTION B

Question 1

Part a.. Explain the steps you would follow in managing the UCC graduation ceremony project. [(10 marks)]
  • Identify Requirements/Needs: Define what the graduation ceremony requires — venue, logistics, catering, audiovisuals, printing of certificates, security, and guest management.
  • Address Stakeholder Needs: Identify and engage all stakeholders (UCC management, graduating students, parents, academic staff, caterers, security, media) and address their needs, concerns, and expectations in planning.
  • Set Up Communications: Establish and maintain effective communications among all active stakeholders, ensuring timely, accurate, and collaborative information sharing throughout.
  • Develop the Project Management Plan: Create detailed plans covering scope, schedule, budget, quality, risk, procurement, and HR — coordinated into one comprehensive PMP.
  • Execute the Plan: Implement the plans: procure services, deploy the project team, manage resources, monitor activities, and handle changes or issues as they arise.
  • Monitor and Control: Track progress against the plan — check that logistics are on schedule, costs are within budget, and quality targets are met. Initiate change requests where needed.
  • Close the Project: After the ceremony, release resources, settle contracts with suppliers, document lessons learned, and prepare a post-event review.
📖 Source: Unit 1, Session 2 — Steps to Manage a Project, p.7
Part b.. Examine three constraints you are likely to encounter with the aid of a diagram and indicate how these constraints will affect the successful execution of the project. [(10 marks)]
  • Scope: Defines all activities — venue setup, rehearsals, ceremony programme, certificates, reception, and media coverage. If additional elements are requested without adjusting time or budget, quality will be compromised.
  • Cost (Budget): The ceremony must be delivered within UCC's approved budget. Uncontrolled spending or scope expansion without a budget increase could exhaust funds before completion.
  • Schedule (Time): The graduation date is fixed and cannot be moved. All procurement, certificate printing, rehearsals, and setup must be completed before that fixed date.
DIAGRAM: The Triple Constraint Triangle
[Triangle — Scope (top), Cost/Budget (bottom-left), Schedule/Time (bottom-right), Quality at centre]
Impact: These constraints are interdependent — extending time increases cost; cutting cost reduces scope; expanding scope requires more time and budget. The project manager must continuously balance all three.
📖 Source: Unit 1, Session 2 — Constraints to Project Management, p.8

Question 2

Part a.. Using relevant examples, examine how the following cost may be applicable to a product launch project: Direct, Indirect, Fixed, Variable, and Expedited costs. [(10 marks)]
  • Direct Cost: Costs clearly assigned to the specific project activity that generated them. Example: All labour costs of the launch event team and all materials (banners, brochures) used exclusively for the product launch.
  • Indirect Cost: Costs linked to overhead and general administration not exclusively attributable to the launch project. Example: Utilities, insurance, depreciation on shared equipment, and general advertising.
  • Fixed Cost: Costs that do not vary with usage throughout the project lifecycle. Example: Lease of the event venue at a fixed fee regardless of actual attendance numbers.
  • Variable Cost: Costs that increase or decrease based on actual activity levels. Example: Catering costs that vary with the number of attendees.
  • Expedited Cost: Unplanned costs incurred to speed up project completion. Example: Paying overtime to event staff or hiring additional temporary workers to meet the launch deadline.
📖 Source: Unit 2, Session 2 — Nature of Project Cost, pp.33–34
Part b.. Examine the three main processes you would use to ensure effective project cost management. [(10 marks)]
  • Project Cost Estimating: Approximating the cost to complete project activities by breaking the project into phases or deliverables. Example: Separately costing materials, labour, and equipment for the launch.
  • Project Cost Budgeting: Aggregating the estimated costs of individual activities to establish an authorized cost baseline. Example: Combining all launch expense estimates into one approved budget.
  • Project Cost Control: Monitoring the status of the project to update costs and manage changes to the cost baseline. Example: Tracking spending against the baseline to catch overruns.
📖 Source: Unit 2, Session 3 — Project Cost Management Process, pp.37–39

Question 3

Part a.. Assess four reasons why the Agenda 111 project must be completed within the stipulated time framework provided. [(10 marks)]
  • Indicator of Success: Time management provides assurance of project success. Missing the deadline is viewed as project failure, signaling poor governance for a government project.
  • Financial Cost of Delays: A critical delay extends overhead costs, including idle labour and material cost escalation due to inflation, prolonging expenditure beyond the approved budget.
  • Service Delivery Obligation: The project aims to improve healthcare in each district. Every day of delay denies citizens access to critical healthcare infrastructure.
  • Contractual/Legal Obligations: Construction contracts include liquidated damages clauses. Exceeding the timeline creates legal and financial liability for the government.
📖 Source: Unit 3, Session 4 — Importance of Time Management, pp.71–72; Unit 1, Session 6 — Project Delays, pp.23–24
Part b.. Explain the steps to be followed in developing a human resource management plan for this project. [(10 marks)]
  • Step 1 — Identify Roles: Define all roles required to achieve the project goals (e.g., civil engineers, quantity surveyors, site supervisors).
  • Step 2 — Specify Skills: Specify the conceptual, human, and technical skills and qualifications expected of each team member.
  • Step 3 — Define Reporting: Draft a project organizational structure showing the chain of command and communication flow between all teams.
  • Step 4 — Staffing Plan: Describe when and how project team members will be acquired, how long they will be needed, and plans for release and training.
📖 Source: Unit 4, Session 2 — Plan and Acquire Human Resource for Projects, pp.93–94

Question 4

Part a.. Define project integration management. [(2 marks)]
Project Integration Management involves the processes and activities to identify, define, combine, unify, and coordinate the various processes and activities within the Project Management Process Groups. It includes making choices about resource allocation and managing interdependencies.
📖 Source: Unit 5, Session 1 — Project Integration Management, p.119
Part b.. Explain the processes involved in project integration management. [(12 marks)]
  • Develop Project Charter: Formally authorizes the existence of the project and provides the project manager authority to apply resources.
  • Develop Project Management Plan: Defines, prepares, and coordinates all subsidiary plans into a comprehensive PMP.
  • Direct and Manage Project Work: Leading and performing the work defined in the PMP and implementing approved changes.
  • Monitor and Control Project Work: Tracking, reviewing, and reporting project progress against performance objectives.
  • Perform Integrated Change Control: Reviewing all change requests and approving or rejecting changes to deliverables and documents.
  • Close Project or Phase: Finalizing all activities to formally complete the project or phase.
📖 Source: Unit 5, Session 1 — Project Integration Management Process, pp.119–120
Part c.. State two importance of project integration management. [(3 marks)]
  • Resource Allocation: Managing integration helps initiate the project by allocating resources to commence work. The project charter directs teams to execute the project plan.
  • Prevents Isolation: Integration management ensures projects are not managed in isolation — it accounts for how aspects of the project relate to each other and the organization.
📖 Source: Unit 5, Session 1 — Importance of Project Integration Management, p.120
Part d.. State two challenges in project integration management. [(3 marks)]
  • Tracking Interdependencies: Keeping track of everything and knowing how one element impacts others is very challenging, especially in large, complex projects.
  • Global Information Dissemination: Projects with teams spread globally face difficulties regarding information dissemination due to noise along the communication channel.
📖 Source: Unit 5, Session 1 — Challenges of Project Integration Management, p.120

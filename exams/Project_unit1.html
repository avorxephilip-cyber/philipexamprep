Unit 1: Nature of Project and Project Management

Self-Assessment Questions & Answers

Session 1: Nature of a Project

1. Define project.
A project is a temporary, planned, complex, one-time, customer-focused endeavor undertaken to create a unique outcome with a limited budget, schedule, and resources.
2. State three (3) examples of a project.
  • Developing a new product, service, or result.
  • Introducing a new product to a new market.
  • Developing or acquiring a new or modified information system (hardware or software).
3. State four (4) characteristics of a project.
  • Objective to be achieved: Every project is an ad hoc endeavor with a specific goal.
  • Schedule to closure: Every project has a temporal life cycle with a specific time to finish.
  • Complexity: Every project has its level of complexity and requires specific technology.
  • Resources needs: Every project requires specific labor, personnel, equipment, materials, and a budget.

Session 2: Project Management Overview

1. State the steps to manage a project.
  • Identification of requirements/needs.
  • Addressing various needs, concerns, and expectations of stakeholders.
  • Setting up and carrying out active, effective communications among stakeholders.
  • Managing stakeholders towards meeting project requirements and creating deliverables.
  • Balancing competing project constraints.
2. State the constraints to project management.
The primary constraints (The Triple Constraint and beyond) include: Time, Cost, Scope, Resources, Risk, and Quality.

Session 3: Project Governance & Roles

1. Define project stakeholders and give four examples.
Stakeholders are individuals and organizations who may have an interest in and may be actively involved in the project. Examples: Owners, Sponsors, Customers, and Contractors.
2. State any three responsibilities of a project manager.
  • Linking strategy and the project (including the team).
  • Reporting and receiving updates regarding the team’s efforts to stay on track.
  • Sharing progress reports with sponsors and all stakeholders.
3. State any three interpersonal skills of a project manager.
Leadership skills, Team building skills, and Motivational skills.

Session 4: Project Conflict and Resolutions

1. Explain any two types of project conflicts and state how you will resolve each.
  • Group Differences: Occurs when workers do not work efficiently together due to individual differences or unhealthy competition. Resolution: Avoidance or Smoothing.
  • Communication: Serious conflicts rooted in the medium, appropriateness, or content of communications. Resolution: Accommodation or Forcing.

Session 5: Project Life Cycle

1. What is project life cycle?
A project life cycle refers to all the phases or stages a project goes through from its initiation to its closeout.
2. State the phases of project life cycle.
  1. Project Initiation
  2. Project Planning
  3. Project Execution and Controls
  4. Project Termination/Closure

Session 6: Project Delays

1. What is project delay?
Project delay is defined as the inability of a project team to complete all stipulated activities within the estimated duration.
2. State any three (3) types of project delays.
Critical or non-critical delays, Excusable or non-excusable delays, and Concurrent delays.
3. State any three (3) causes of project delays.
Force majeure clauses (natural calamities), delays from the client regarding approvals/decisions, and resource shortages.

Unit 6: Project Closure and Ethics

Self-Assessment Questions & Answers

Session 1: Project Closing Processes

1. State the key activities in project closure.
  • Gaining final acceptance from the customer or sponsor.
  • Conducting a post-project or phase-end review.
  • Recording the impact of tailoring to any process.
  • Documenting lessons learned.
  • Archiving all relevant project documents in the Project Management Information System (PMIS).
  • Releasing project resources (personnel, equipment, etc.).

Session 2: Administrative and Contract Closure

1. Explain the difference between Administrative Closure and Contract Closure.
  • Administrative Closure: The internal process of closing the project within the performing organization (releasing staff, archiving files, final reports).
  • Contract Closure: The process of completing and settling each contract, including the resolution of any open items and the formal acceptance of deliverables by the buyer.

Session 3: Lessons Learned and Knowledge Management

1. State the importance of a Lessons Learned register.
The lessons learned register is crucial for continuous improvement. It provides future project teams with information on what worked well and what didn't, helping to avoid past mistakes and replicate successful strategies in future projects.

Session 4: Professional and Social Responsibility

1. Define Professional Responsibility in project management.
Professional responsibility involves the obligation of project managers to conduct themselves in a manner that is ethical, legal, and professional, ensuring they act in the best interest of the stakeholders and the profession at large.
2. State the four pillars of the PMI Code of Ethics.
  • Responsibility
  • Respect
  • Fairness
  • Honesty

Session 5: Ethics in Project Management

1. Explain "Conflict of Interest" in a project context.
A conflict of interest occurs when a project manager's personal interests or relationships interfere, or appear to interfere, with their ability to make objective decisions that are in the best interest of the project or organization.

Session 6: Cultural Diversity and Global Projects

1. State two (2) challenges of managing global projects.
  • Time Zone Differences: Coordinating communication and meetings across different geographical locations.
  • Cultural Differences: Navigating varying work ethics, communication styles, and business practices across different countries.
2. State the importance of cultural awareness.
Cultural awareness helps project managers build stronger, more inclusive teams, reduces misunderstandings, and fosters a collaborative environment that respects the diverse backgrounds of all project participants.

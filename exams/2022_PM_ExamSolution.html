2022 Project Management Exam Solutions

2022 EXAMINATION — SECTION B

Question 1

You have been appointed as the project manager for the next graduation ceremony of the University of Cape Coast. Required: A.. Explain the steps you would follow in managing this project. [(10 marks)] Model Answer: Identify Requirements/Needs: Define what the graduation ceremony requires — venue, logistics, catering, audiovisuals, printing of certificates, security, and guest management. Address Stakeholder Needs: Identify and engage all stakeholders (UCC management, graduating students, parents, academic staff, caterers, security, media) and address their needs, concerns, and expectations in planning. Set Up Communications: Establish and maintain effective communications among all active stakeholders, ensuring timely, accurate, and collaborative information sharing throughout. Develop the Project Management Plan: Create detailed plans covering scope, schedule, budget, quality, risk, procurement, and HR — coordinated into one comprehensive PMP. Execute the Plan: Implement the plans: procure services, deploy the project team, manage resources, monitor activities, and handle changes or issues as they arise. Monitor and Control: Track progress against the plan — check that logistics are on schedule, costs are within budget, and quality targets are met. Initiate change requests where needed. Close the Project: After the ceremony, release resources, settle contracts with suppliers, document lessons learned, and prepare a post-event review. 📖 Source: Unit 1, Session 2 — Steps to Manage a Project, p.7 B.. Examine three constraints you are likely to encounter with the aid of a diagram and indicate how these constraints will affect the successful execution of the project. [(10 marks)] Model Answer: Scope: Defines all activities — venue setup, rehearsals, ceremony programme, certificates, reception, and media coverage. If additional elements are requested without adjusting time or budget, quality will be compromised and the ceremony may not be delivered as planned. Cost (Budget): The ceremony must be delivered within UCC's approved budget. Uncontrolled spending or scope expansion without a budget increase could exhaust funds before the ceremony is complete. Schedule (Time): The graduation date is fixed and cannot be moved. All procurement, certificate printing, rehearsals, and setup must be completed before that fixed date — any delay in one activity compresses the remaining tasks. DIAGRAM: [Triangle — Scope (top), Cost/Budget (bottom-left), Schedule/Time (bottom-right), Quality at centre] Impact: These constraints are interdependent — extending time increases cost; cutting cost reduces scope; expanding scope requires more time and budget. The project manager must continuously balance all three. 📖 Source: Unit 1, Session 2 — Constraints to Project Management, p.8

Question 2

A company has consulted to manage the launch of a new product. As the project manager for the event, you are required to address the following: A.. Using relevant examples, examine how the following cost may be applicable to the project: i. Direct cost ii. Indirect cost iii. Fixed cost iv. Variable cost v. Expedited cost [(10 marks)] Model Answer: Direct Cost: Costs clearly assigned to the specific project activity that generated them. Example: All labour costs of the launch event team and all materials (banners, brochures, demo units) used exclusively for the product launch. Indirect Cost: Costs linked to overhead and general administration not exclusively attributable to the launch project. Example: Utilities, insurance, depreciation on shared equipment, secretarial support, and general advertising. Fixed Cost: Costs that do not vary with usage throughout the project lifecycle. Example: Lease of the event venue at a fixed fee regardless of actual attendance numbers. Variable Cost: Costs that increase or decrease based on actual activity levels. Example: Catering costs that vary with the number of attendees, or printed materials that vary with the guest list size. Expedited Cost: Unplanned costs incurred to speed up project completion. Example: Paying overtime to event staff or hiring additional temporary workers to meet the launch deadline after falling behind schedule. 📖 Source: Unit 2, Session 2 — Nature of Project Cost, pp.33–34 B.. Examine the three main processes you would use to ensure effective project cost management. [(10 marks)] Model Answer: (Cost Estimating, Cost Budgeting, Cost Control — see full answers under 2019 Question 2b.)

Question 3

The government of Ghana is putting up a three-storey building as part of its Agenda 111 projects. This project is expected to be completed within three years. You have been appointed as the project consultant. Required: A.. Assess four reasons why this project must be completed within the stipulated time framework provided. [(10 marks)] Model Answer: Time as an Indicator of Project Success: Project time management provides assurance of project success — missing the deadline is viewed as project failure. For a government project (Agenda 111), a missed deadline signals poor governance and budget inefficiency. Financial Cost of Delays: A critical delay extends overhead costs, including idle labour, equipment costs, and material cost escalation due to inflation. Prolonging the project increases expenditure beyond the approved budget, requiring supplementary funding. Service Delivery Obligation: The project aims to build hospitals to improve healthcare in each district. Every day of delay denies citizens access to critical healthcare infrastructure — a social obligation that makes timely delivery imperative. Contractual and Legal Obligations: Construction contracts include liquidated damages clauses imposing financial penalties for every day of delay beyond the agreed completion date. Exceeding the timeline creates legal and financial liability for the government. 📖 Source: Unit 3, Session 4 — Importance of Time Management, pp.71–72; Unit 1, Session 6 — Project Delays, pp.23–24 B.. Explain the steps to be followed in developing a human resource management plan for this project. [(10 marks)] Model Answer: Step 1 — Identify and Document Roles and Responsibilities: Define all roles required to achieve the project goals (e.g., civil engineers, quantity surveyors, project coordinators, site supervisors, health facility designers). Document the responsibilities attached to each role. Step 2 — Specify Skills Requirements: After determining roles, specify the conceptual, human, and technical skills and qualifications expected of each team member to effectively handle their responsibilities. Step 3 — Define Reporting Relationships: Draft a project organizational structure showing the chain of command and communication flow between all teams. This enables clear reporting lines and prevents confusion on authority. Step 4 — Create a Staffing Management Plan: Describe when and how project team members will be acquired, how long they will be needed, and plans for staff acquisition, release, training needs, and recognition/rewards systems. 📖 Source: Unit 4, Session 2 — Plan and Acquire Human Resource for Projects, pp.93–94 Question 4 (Exam Code 01) B.. Using relevant examples, distinguish between the following types of cost: i. direct and indirect project costs ii. Fixed and variable costs iii. normal and expedited costs iv. recurring and nonrecurring costs [(2.5 marks each)] Model Answer:
Part i. Direct vs Indirect Project Costs: Direct costs are clearly assigned to the specific project activity that generated them — e.g., direct labour wages of workers on the project, direct materials incorporated into the deliverable. Indirect costs are linked to overhead and general administration, not traceable to any single project activity — e.g., utilities, insurance, depreciation, advertising, and secretarial support. ii. Fixed vs Variable Costs: Fixed costs do not vary with usage throughout the project lifecycle — e.g., leasing capital equipment or project hardware at a fixed monthly rate. Variable costs increase or decrease throughout the project lifecycle, subject to significant fluctuations — e.g., equipment usage costs that vary with the number of hours used. iii. Normal vs Expedited Costs: Normal costs are incurred in the routine process of completing the project according to the original planned schedule agreed upon by all stakeholders. Expedited costs are unplanned costs incurred when steps are taken to speed up the project's completion — e.g., crashing activities through overtime, hiring additional temporary workers, or contracting external resources to regain lost time. iv. Recurring vs Nonrecurring Costs: Recurring costs are continuous in nature throughout the project lifecycle — e.g., labour costs, material costs, logistics, and sales costs. Nonrecurring costs are charged only once at the beginning or closure of the project — e.g., preliminary marketing analysis, personnel training, and outplacement services.
(Source: Unit 2, Session 2 — Nature of Project Cost, pp.33–34)

CORPORATE FINANCIAL ANALYSIS — 2025 EXAM

Question 1 (Compulsory)

AA Limited operates in a highly competitive market and is considering introducing a new product to expand its current range. The new product will require the purchase of a specialised machine costing GH₵825,000. The machine has a useful life of four years and is expected to have a scrap value at the end of Year 4 of GH₵50,000. The company uses the straight-line method of depreciation. The machine would be used exclusively for the new product.

Due to a shortage of space in the factory, investment in the new machine would necessitate the disposal, for GH₵80,000, of an existing machine which has a net book value of GH₵69,000. This machine, if retained for a further year, would have earned a contribution of GH₵100,000 before being scrapped for nil value. The machine had a zero-tax written down value and therefore there will be no effect on tax depreciation arising from the disposal of the machine.

The company employed the services of a consultant, at a cost of GH₵120,000, to determine the demand for the new product.

The consultant's estimated demand is given below:

The new product is expected to earn a contribution of GH₵50 per unit. Fixed costs of GH₵380,000 per annum, excluding depreciation of the new machine, will arise as a direct result of manufacture. The new machine will attract an additional maintenance fee of GH₵20,000 each year.

Taxation

  • Tax depreciation: 20% per annum on the reducing balance, with a balancing adjustment in the year of disposal.
  • Taxation rate: 30% of taxable profits. Tax is payable in the year in which it arises.

Other information

  • Weighted average cost of capital (WACC): 15% per annum
  • Growth rate after the horizon period: 5%

Required:
Using the free cash flow model, estimate the value of AA Limited. [20 marks]

Solution to Question 1

Step 1 — Identifying Relevant and Irrelevant Costs

Before building the cash flow model, we must apply the golden rule of investment appraisal: only incremental, future cash flows are relevant.

ItemTreatmentReason
Consultant fee — GH₵120,000 EXCLUDED (Sunk Cost) Already paid — cannot be recovered regardless of the decision. Has no bearing on future cash flows.
Disposal proceeds — GH₵80,000 INCLUDED at T₀ (Inflow) A real incremental cash inflow — only arising because the project is proceeding.
Tax on disposal gain INCLUDED at T₀ (Outflow) Gain = GH₵80,000 − GH₵69,000 = GH₵11,000. Tax = 30% × GH₵11,000 = GH₵3,300.
Lost contribution — GH₵100,000 INCLUDED Year 1 (Opportunity Cost) By disposing of the old machine now, one year's contribution is sacrificed. Treated as a gross incremental cost in Year 1.
NBV of old machine — GH₵69,000 EXCLUDED A sunk (book) value — not a future cash flow. The disposal proceeds (GH₵80,000) are what matter.

Step 3 — Working 2: Tax Depreciation Schedule (Reducing Balance @ 20%)

For tax purposes, capital allowances are claimed on the reducing balance method at 20% per annum — not the straight-line method used in the management accounts. Tax depreciation drives the taxable profit calculation. In Year 4, a balancing allowance is applied: WDV GH₵422,400 − Scrap Value GH₵50,000 = GH₵372,400, ensuring full tax relief in the final year.

Step 6 — Terminal Value Using the Gordon Growth Model

After the 4-year forecast period, future cash flows are assumed to grow at a perpetual rate of 5%. The Gordon Growth Model converts this into a single terminal value at the end of Year 4:

Terminal Value = CF₄ × (1 + g) ÷ (r − g)

= GH₵756,720 × (1 + 0.05) ÷ (0.15 − 0.05)
= GH₵756,720 × 1.05 ÷ 0.10
= GH₵794,556 ÷ 0.10
= GH₵7,945,560

Conclusion

Using the Free Cash Flow model, the estimated value of AA Limited is GH₵5,215,028. This represents the present value of all future free cash flows the firm is expected to generate, discounted at its WACC of 15%.

  • The consultant's fee of GH₵120,000 was correctly excluded as a sunk cost.
  • The lost contribution of GH₵100,000 was treated as a gross opportunity cost in Year 1.
  • Tax of GH₵3,300 on the disposal gain of GH₵11,000 was applied at T₀.
  • The Gordon Growth Model captured perpetual value beyond Year 4 using a 5% sustainable growth rate.

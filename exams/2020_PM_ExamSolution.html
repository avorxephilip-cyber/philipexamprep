2020 Project Management Exam Solutions

2020 EXAMINATION — SECTION B

Question 1

Part a.. Explain the key responsibility of the project manager in defining project scope. [[2 Marks]]
The project manager's primary responsibility in scope management is to ensure that the project includes all the work required, and only the work required, to complete the project successfully. They must ensure that every deliverable is accurately defined so that it can be completed within the allotted time and budget.
📖 Source: Unit 3, Session 1 — Introduction to Project Scope Management, p.55
Part b.. Explain four common problems associated with project scope management. [[8 Marks]]
  • Ambiguity: Leads to unnecessary work and confusion. Scope must be precise to avoid misinterpretations.
  • Incomplete Definition: Leads to schedule slips and cost overruns when deliverables are missed.
  • Transience (Scope Creep): Unstable scopes lead to late deliveries and never-ending projects.
  • Uncollaborated Scope: Scope not agreed upon by all stakeholders causes misinterpretations in requirements.
📖 Source: Unit 3, Session 1 — Common Problems in Project Scope Management, p.56
Part c.. Explain any five importance of project scope management. [[10 marks]]
  • Shared Understanding: Ensures stakeholders and team members agree on how goals will be met.
  • Verification: Sets up procedures for verifying and approving completed work.
  • Controls Scope Bloat: Helps avoid challenges with unruly requirements lists.
  • Defines Exclusions: Clearly states what is NOT part of the project to prevent execution errors.
  • Enables Accurate Estimation: Essential for determining accurate project cost and time.
📖 Source: Unit 3, Session 1 — Importance of Project Scope Management, pp.55-56

Question 2

Part a.. State the four main types of project termination. [[4 Marks]]
  • Starvation: Termination due to budget cuts or lack of continued funding.
  • Extinction: Termination when the project is successfully completed and deliverables are accepted.
  • Addition: Termination where the project becomes a permanent part of the parent organization.
  • Integration: Termination where project resources and outputs are distributed throughout the organization.
📖 Source: Unit 1, Session 5 — Project Termination, p.20
Part b.. Using relevant examples, explain the three main processes used to ensure effective project costs management. [[6 marks]]
  • Cost Estimating: Approximating the cost to complete project activities. Example: Separately costing foundation, roofing, and finishing for a house.
  • Cost Budgeting: Aggregating estimated costs to establish an authorized baseline. Example: Combining all civil and MEP costs into one approved budget.
  • Cost Control: Monitoring project status to update costs and manage changes to the baseline. Example: Tracking monthly spending against the budget.
📖 Source: Unit 2, Session 3 — Project Cost Management Process, pp.37–39
Part c.. Explain any five sources of long-term project funding. [[10 marks)]
  • Sale and Leaseback: Selling assets to a financial institution and leasing them back to release capital.
  • Loan Capital (Debentures): Loans secured against company assets where interest is paid before dividends.
  • Business Angels: Private investors who provide funds in exchange for an equity stake.
  • Venture Capital: Substantial funds for high-risk companies in exchange for strategic equity.
  • Share Capital: Raising funds through ordinary or preference shareholders.
📖 Source: Unit 2, Session 4 — Sources of Long-Term Project Funding, pp.42–44

Question 3

Part a.. All projects are temporary in nature. Discuss [[4 Marks]]
This statement is fundamentally correct. A project is a temporary planned endeavor with a specific start and end point.

However, 'temporary' does not mean short duration (some span years) or short-lived outcomes (e.g., a bridge). It refers to the project's engagement and team formation. Projects end when objectives are achieved, the need vanishes, or support is withdrawn. This distinguishes projects from continuous operations.
📖 Source: Unit 1, Session 1 — Nature of a Project, p.3
Part b.. Explain any six characteristics of a project [[6 Marks]]
  • Objective to Achieve: An ad hoc endeavor with a specific goal.
  • Schedule to Closure: A temporal life cycle for activity accomplishment.
  • Complexity: Requires specific technology and skills.
  • Resource Needs: Requires labour, equipment, materials, and budget.
  • Organizational Structure: Management structure for controls and direction.
  • Risk and Safety Controls: Compliance with standards and stakeholder needs.
📖 Source: Unit 1, Session 1 — Characteristics of a Project, pp.4–5
Part c.. With the aid of a diagram, explain the three most important traditional constraints of project management [[6 Marks]]
  • Scope: All the work required for delivery.
  • Cost (Budget): The allocated financial resources.
  • Schedule (Time): The planned timeline for completion.

Question 4

Part a.. State two importance of human resource planning in project management [[4 Marks]]
  • Identifies Roles: Provides clarity on recruitment needs and task orientation.
  • Sets Skills Standards: Prevents mismatching of qualifications and guides recruitment.
📖 Source: Unit 4, Session 2 — Importance of Human Resource Planning, p.94
Part b.. Explain any four functions performed by the project manager in managing and leading project teams [[8 Marks]]
  • Influencing the Team: Managing factors like geographical locations and internal politics.
  • Building Trust: Fostering familiarity and cooperation among team members.
  • Performance Appraisals: Providing feedback for individual and team improvement.
  • Conflict Management: Resolving inevitable conflicts using various resolution techniques.
📖 Source: Unit 4, Session 3 — Manage Project Team, pp.100–101
Part c.. State the steps to consider in establishing the project risk context [[8 Marks]]
  • Step 1: Establish the organizational and project environment.
  • Step 2: Specify the main objectives and outcomes required.
  • Step 3: Identify success criteria for measuring risk consequences.
  • Step 4: Define key elements for structuring risk identification.
📖 Source: Unit 6, Session 2 — Establish the Risk Context, p.153

Unit 4: Cost and Risk Management

Self-Assessment Questions & Answers

Session 1: Project Cost Management

1. Define project cost management.
Project Cost Management includes the processes involved in planning, estimating, budgeting, financing, funding, managing, and controlling costs so that the project can be completed within the approved budget.
2. State the processes of project cost management.
  • Plan Cost Management
  • Estimate Costs
  • Determine Budget
  • Control Costs

Session 2: Project Cost Estimation Techniques

1. State any three (3) importance of cost estimation.
  • Funding Basis: It provides a basis for project funding and budget allocation.
  • Economic Feasibility: It helps in evaluating the economic feasibility of the project.
  • Control Baseline: It provides a baseline for project cost control and performance measurement.
2. Explain the difference between Analogous and Parametric estimating.
  • Analogous Estimating: Uses the values of parameters from a previous, similar project (e.g., cost, budget, duration) as the basis for estimating the same parameter for a current project. It is generally less costly but also less accurate.
  • Parametric Estimating: Uses a statistical relationship between historical data and other variables (e.g., square footage in construction) to calculate an estimate for activity parameters.

Session 3: Project Budgeting

1. Define project budget.
A project budget is the sum of the estimated costs of individual activities or work packages to establish an authorized cost baseline.
2. State the difference between Management Reserve and Contingency Reserve.
  • Contingency Reserve: Budget within the cost baseline that is allocated for identified risks that are accepted ("known-unknowns").
  • Management Reserve: An amount of the project budget withheld for management control purposes for unforeseen work that is within scope ("unknown-unknowns"). It is not part of the cost baseline.

Session 4: Project Risk Management

1. Define project risk management.
Project Risk Management includes the processes of conducting risk management planning, identification, analysis, response planning, and controlling risk on a project.
2. State the processes of project risk management.
  • Plan Risk Management
  • Identify Risks
  • Perform Qualitative Risk Analysis
  • Perform Quantitative Risk Analysis
  • Plan Risk Responses
  • Control Risks

Session 5: Risk Identification and Analysis

1. State any three (3) tools used for risk identification.
  • Brainstorming
  • Checklist analysis
  • SWOT Analysis: (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
2. Define Qualitative and Quantitative Risk Analysis.
  • Qualitative Analysis: The process of prioritizing risks for further analysis or action by assessing and combining their probability of occurrence and impact.
  • Quantitative Analysis: The process of numerically analyzing the effect of identified risks on overall project objectives.

Session 6: Risk Response Strategies

1. State the strategies for negative risks (threats).
  • Avoid: Changing the project plan to eliminate the threat.
  • Transfer: Shifting the impact of a threat to a third party (e.g., insurance).
  • Mitigate: Reducing the probability or impact of a threat.
  • Accept: Acknowledging the risk and not taking action unless it occurs.
2. State the strategies for positive risks (opportunities).
  • Exploit: Ensuring the opportunity is realized.
  • Enhance: Increasing the probability or positive impacts of an opportunity.
  • Share: Allocating ownership to a third party who is best able to capture the opportunity.
  • Accept: Being willing to take advantage of the opportunity if it arises.

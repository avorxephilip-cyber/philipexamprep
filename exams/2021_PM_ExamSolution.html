2021 Project Management Exam Solutions

2021 EXAMINATION — SECTION B

Question 1

Part a.. Using relevant examples, explain the four main classifications of project delay types. [(10 marks)]
  • Critical vs Non-Critical Delays: A critical delay extends the overall project duration, leading to extended overhead and liquidated damages. A non-critical delay causes individual activities to complete later but does not extend the total project duration. Example: Delay in foundation work (critical) vs. delay in landscaping (non-critical).
  • Excusable vs Non-Excusable Delays: Excusable delays are outside the team's control (e.g., force majeure). Non-excusable delays result from the team's own failures. Example: A flood (excusable) vs. failing to mobilize on time (non-excusable).
  • Concurrent Delays: Occur when more than one delay event affects multiple activities simultaneously. Example: Equipment failure and material shortage occurring at the same time.
  • Compensable vs Non-Compensable Delays: Compensable delays entitle the manager to time and cost compensation (usually client-caused). Non-compensable means the manager is at fault. Example: Client design changes (compensable) vs. poor scheduling (non-compensable).
📖 Source: Unit 1, Session 6 — Types of Delays in Projects, pp.23–24
Part b.. Examine five causes of delays in projects. [(10 marks)]
  • Design Errors: Errors requiring rework or demolition. Example: A structural design error in a bridge.
  • Scope Change: Mid-execution changes impacting all baselines. Example: Client requesting additional floors mid-construction.
  • Inappropriate Procurement: Vague contracts leading to disputes. Example: Unclear road construction contracts stalling work.
  • Complexity: Large projects vulnerable to inflation and price spikes. Example: International material price spikes on a dam project.
  • Subcontractor Delays: Specialized partners failing to deliver on time. Example: MEP subcontractor failing to complete electrical work.
📖 Source: Unit 1, Session 6 — Causes of Project Delays, pp.25–27

Question 2

Part a.. Analyse the processes involved in project procurement management. [(12 marks)]
  • Procurement Planning: Identifying which project needs are best met externally and determining contract types.
  • Solicitation Planning: Preparing solicitation documents like RFPs and RFQs.
  • Solicitation: Obtaining bids and proposals from prospective sellers.
  • Source Selection: Evaluating bids and signing contracts with selected suppliers.
  • Contract Administration: Ensuring the supplier's performance meets contractual requirements.
  • Contract Closeout: Satisfactorily finishing work, making final payments, and documenting lessons learned.
📖 Source: Unit 2, Session 5 — Project Procurement Management, pp.45–47
Part b.. Examine any four main risks that occur in the project procurement process. [(8 marks)]
  • Demand Fluctuation: Demand being higher or lower than projected due to seasonality or price.
  • Vague Price Information: Unstable prices of materials and services affecting total project cost.
  • Unreliable Yield: Supplier production capability failing to meet requirements.
  • Uncertain Lead Time: Lead time uncertainty increasing total cost and reducing service levels.
📖 Source: Unit 2, Session 6 — Procurement Risk Management, pp.49–51

Question 3

Part a.. Examine two importance and challenges each of project communication management. [(8 marks)]
Two Importances:
  • Relay of Information: Ensures appropriate information reaches the right people through the right media.
  • Clarity of Tasks: Ensures all project teams have their tasks clearly communicated.
Two Challenges:
  • Communication During Adversity: Absence of a dynamic plan when issues arise.
  • Language and Cultural Barriers: Differing cultural styles and lack of common language in global projects.
📖 Source: Unit 4, Session 4 — Project Communication Management, pp.104–105
Part b.. Explain the activities involved in the project integration management process. [(12 marks)]
  • Develop Project Charter: Formally authorizes the existence of the project.
  • Develop Project Management Plan: Coordinates all subsidiary plans into a comprehensive PMP.
  • Direct and Manage Project Work: Leading and performing the work defined in the PMP.
  • Monitor and Control Project Work: Tracking and reviewing progress against performance objectives.
  • Perform Integrated Change Control: Reviewing and approving/rejecting all change requests.
  • Close Project or Phase: Finalizing all activities to formally complete the project.
📖 Source: Unit 5, Sessions 1–3 — Project Integration Management Activities, pp.119–132

Question 4

Part a.. Examine four common problems associated with project scope management. [(10 Marks)]
  • Ambiguity: Ambiguity in scope leads to unnecessary work and confusion.
  • Incomplete Definition: Incomplete scopes lead to schedule slips and cost overruns.
  • Transience (Scope Creep): Transient or unstable scopes lead to scope creep — late deliveries and never-ending projects.
  • Uncollaborated Scope: A scope not collaboratively prepared causes misinterpretations in requirements and design.
📖 Source: Unit 3, Session 1 — Common Problems in Project Scope Management, p.56
Part b.. Explain four importance of time management in projects. [(10 marks)]
  • Assurance of Success: Time management provides assurance of project success; missing deadlines is seen as failure.
  • Effectiveness and Efficiency: Ensures activities are performed within the stipulated timeframe and resources are well utilized.
  • Reduces Stress: Proper task identification and timing allocation reduces project-related stress.
  • Quality Output: Ensuring activities follow the schedule prevents haphazard work under pressure, resulting in higher quality.
📖 Source: Unit 3, Session 4 — Importance of Time Management, pp.71–72

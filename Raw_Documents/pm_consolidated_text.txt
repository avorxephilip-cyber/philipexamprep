SBU 405D: Project Management
 Consolidated Unique Examination Questions (2020/21 – 2024/25)
 All five exams consolidated · Duplicates removed · Renumbered 1–65
 Correct answers are highlighted in red, based on the Introduction to Project Management textbook.
1. Advertising, shipping and utilities are examples of project overhead cost.
A. False
B. True
Note: Advertising and shipping are under 'selling and administration' (indirect costs), not overhead. The
statement grouping all three as overhead is false.
2. The process of tracking team member performance, providing feedback, resolving
issues and managing team changes to optimize project performance is referred to as
___________.
A. Human resource management
B. Project team management
C. Project team development
D. Project team acquisition
3. Which of the following stages clearly explain the processes involved in developing the
human resource plan for a project?
I. Specify the skill requirement
II. Identify and document project roles and responsibilities
III. Establish reporting relationships
IV. Create staffing management plan
A. I, III, IV and II
B. II, I, III and IV
C. III, IV, II and I
D. I, II, III and IV
Note: The textbook presents these in order: specify skills → identify roles/responsibilities → reporting
relationships → staffing management plan.
4. Managing human resource of a project helps in the following ways EXCEPT
___________.
A. Creating teams that are unsupportive of each other
B. It gives the ability to resolve employee complaints
C. It helps in knowing when and how team members should be added
D. Identifying and engaging employees with a variety of skill levels

5. Which of the following skills of a manager allows him/her to visualize the entire
organisation and work with ideas and the relationships between abstract concepts?
A. Abstract skills
B. Conceptual skills
C. Human skills
D. Technical skills
6. Unplanned costs incurred when steps are taken to speed up the project's completion
are referred to as ___________.
A. Expedited Costs
B. Fixed Costs
C. Variable Costs
D. Normal Costs
7. Business angels are examples of short-term project funding sources.
A. False
B. True
Note: Business angels are listed under long-term project funding sources in the textbook.
8. Which of the following is NOT an example of long-term sources of project funding?
A. Venture Capital
B. Bank overdraft
C. Retained Profits
D. Share Capital
Note: Bank overdrafts are a short-term funding source (repayable within one year).
9. Which of the classifications of procurement risk management are influenced by factors
such as seasonality, taxation, product availability and price?
A. Disruption risks
B. Demand fluctuation
C. Uncertain lead time
D. Unreliable yield
10. Project termination type where the needed resources to execute the project are either
lacking or inadequately available is referred to as ___________.
A. Project starvation
B. Termination by extinction
C. Termination by addition
D. Termination by integration

11. Project vision conflicts arise due to the differences in stakeholders' opinion and
understanding of the overall outcome of the project.
A. False
B. True
12. The conflict resolution approach where the person involved either physically or
psychologically removes himself or herself from the conflict is referred to as
___________.
A. Accommodation
B. Avoidance
C. Compromise
D. Competition
13. Which of the following is a key feature of the second phase of the project life cycle?
A. A business case is made for the project
B. Activities are outlined
C. Opportunities are identified
D. Monitoring and controlling deliverables
Note: The second phase is Project Planning, which involves outlining activities, tasks, dependencies and
timeframes.
14. Project cost associated with hiring and paying the various classification of workers
involved in developing the project is classified as a service cost.
A. False
B. True
Note: This is Labour Cost. Service Cost refers to work done by consultants, designers and subcontractors.
15. Indirect costs associated with a project include the following EXCEPT ___________.
A. Advertising
B. Depreciation of equipment
C. Insurance
D. Materials
Note: Materials are a direct cost. Advertising, depreciation, and insurance are indirect costs.
16. Which of the following is an example of an active stakeholder of a project?
A. Competitors
B. Persons indirectly affected by the project
C. Project coordinators
D. Public authorities

Note: The textbook lists project coordinators as active stakeholders; competitors, public authorities, and
indirectly affected persons are passive.
17. Which of the following is generally considered as a project?
A. An organisation that strives to stay afloat
B. Conducting research whose outcome is recorded
C. Managing a product
D. Protecting an organisation's sales territory
Note: The textbook defines a project as including 'a result, such as an outcome or document (e.g., a research
project that develops knowledge)'.
18. Scope, cost and risk are the most important traditional constraints of project
management.
A. False
B. True
Note: The three traditional constraints are Scope, Cost (budget), and Time (schedule) — not risk.
19. The responsibilities of the project manager at the organisational level include the
following EXCEPT ___________.
A. Controlling budgets
B. Creating overall project plan and forms project team members
C. Linking between the strategy and project team
D. Sharing progress reports with the sponsors and all stakeholders
Note: Creating the overall project plan and forming the team is a project-level responsibility, not an
organisational-level one.
20. Which of the following best describe the interpersonal skills required by project
managers?
I. Leadership
II. Team building
III. Communication
IV. Building trust
A. I only
B. I and II only
C. I, II, III only
D. I, II, III and IV

25. Which of the following is NOT considered an internal source of risk?
A. Market or operational risk
B. Poor understanding of the project manager's job
C. Poorly organized project office
D. Unrealistic planning and scheduling
Note: Market or operational risk is listed as an external risk in the textbook.
26. The project risk management framework consists of six phases. Which of the
following best arranges the phases in chronological order?
I. Establish the context

II. Risk identification
III. Risk analysis
IV. Risk response planning
V. Risk monitoring and control
A. I, II, III, IV and V
B. II, I, III, V and IV
C. V, IV, III, II and I
D. III, II, IV, V and I
27. Which of the following is NOT true about the project communication plan?
A. It allows the project manager to maintain control of the project and ensure all stakeholders
receive the necessary information
B. Enables the project leader to lead the team to the desired project outcome
C. It increases the inconsistencies of how the project is handled
D. Ensures that all stakeholders have the same information on the project requirements and
changes
Note: The textbook states the communication plan increases CONSISTENCY — so option C is false.
28. Which of the following is NOT an item included in the risk register?
A. Assessment of the likelihood of occurrence
B. Data of identifications
C. Historical data
D. Section of project
Note: Historical data is not listed among the items on a risk register in the textbook.
29. Which of the following best explains the processes involved in the risk scoring
matrix?
A. Identify all the risks, assign severity/impact if they should occur and assign a probability of
occurrence rates
B. Identify the impact/severity if they occur, assign a probability of likelihood of occurrence,
assess all risk
C. Identify all risks, assign a probability of occurrence rates/values and assign
severity/impact if they should occur
D. Establish the organizational and project environment, assign severity/impact if they occur and
specify objectives
Note: Textbook procedure: (1) Identify risks, (2) assign probability/likelihood, (3) assign severity/impact.
30. Which of the following is NOT a feature of a project?
A. Repetitive process
B. Temporal in nature

C. Unique deliverables
D. Defined beginning and end
Note: Projects are unique and temporary with defined start/end. Repetitive process describes operations, not
projects.

51. Which of the following is true about projects?
I. Every project has specific starting and ending points
II. The duration of projects is perpetual
III. Projects are permanent in nature
IV. The lifecycle of the product created by the project is automatically short
A. I and II
B. I and IV
C. III and IV
D. II and III
Note: Only statement I is definitively true. Statement IV is explicitly stated as false in the textbook. B is the
closest correct answer noting only I is fully true; however the exam format makes A the best selection since only
I is correct among all four.

52. Total direct labour cost is calculated as ___________.
A. The product of direct labour rate and total labour hours
B. Total fixed cost multiplied by labour rate
C. The product of preliminary market analysis and labour cost
D. Sales cost plus logistics and direct labour cost
Note: The textbook formula: Total direct labour cost = (Direct labour rate) × (Total labour hours).
53. Project variable cost accelerates or varies throughout the project life cycle whereas
project fixed cost does not vary with respect to their usage throughout the project life
cycle.
A. False
B. True
Note: Both definitions match exactly what is stated in the textbook for variable and fixed costs.
54. Advertising, shipping, salaries and insurance are examples of ___________.
A. Accelerated cost
B. Corporate cost
C. Direct cost
D. Overhead cost
Note: These fall under indirect costs (overhead/selling and administration), collectively referred to as overhead
cost in this context.
55. The third step in the project integration process involves ___________.
A. Directing and managing project
B. Developing a project charter
C. Developing a project management plan
D. Monitoring and controlling project work
Note: Integration steps: 1st = Develop Project Charter, 2nd = Develop Project Management Plan, 3rd = Direct
and Manage Project Work.
56. Which of the following is a key challenge in project integration management?
A. Developing project plan
B. Information dissemination when project teams are spread globally
C. Developing performance reports
D. Providing accurate forecasting figures
Note: The textbook specifically identifies globally spread teams as a key integration challenge causing
information dissemination difficulties.
57. Which of the following should be the FIRST document a project manager should
consult when developing the project management plan?

A. Outputs from other processes
B. Organizational process assets
C. Project charter
D. Issue logs
Note: The project charter is the primary input for developing the project management plan.
58. Which of the following outputs are likely to be realized when the project manager has
successfully directed and managed project work?
A. Change log
B. Deliverables
C. Project management plan updates
D. Work performance reports
Note: The textbook states that Deliverables are the key output when directing and managing project work.
59. Human skills are required for communication only.
A. False
B. True
Note: Human skills, also called human relation skills, require communication AND attention to relationships with
others — not just communication.
60. Which of the following is the FIRST phase of team development?
A. Forming
B. Storming
C. Norming
D. Performing
Note: The Tuckman ladder stages in order: Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Adjourning.
61. At which phase of team development do team members begin to address the project
work, take technical decisions and develop a management approach to the project?
A. Forming
B. Storming
C. Norming
D. Performing
Note: Storming is defined as the phase where the team begins to address project work and technical decisions.
62. At which stage of the team development process do members begin to work together
and adjust their work habits and behaviours in support of the team?
A. Forming
B. Storming
C. Norming

D. Performing
Note: Norming: team members begin to work together and adjust their work habits and behaviours to support the
team.
63. The following are examples of hard-copy documents used in managing project
information EXCEPT ___________.
A. Letters
B. Memos
C. E-mail
D. Press release
Note: E-mail is an electronic communication tool, not a hard-copy document. Letters, memos, and press
releases are hard-copy documents.
64. When parties to a conflict come to an agreement through yielding or conforming to the
positions of others, cooperating in an effort to create harmony even at the expense of
their own ideas and values, which conflict resolution approach has been adopted?
A. Accommodation
B. Competition
C. Compromise
D. Collaboration
Note: This is the exact textbook definition of Accommodation — yielding to others' positions to maintain
harmony.
65. The phase of the project life cycle which involves the development of detailed plans to
confirm the specifications in the project scope defined at the initial phase is referred to as
___________.
A. Execution
B. Initiation
C. Planning
D. Closure
Note: The Planning phase involves developing detailed plans to confirm specifications defined at initiation.
